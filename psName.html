OT:8034 shem (shame);

A primitive word [perhaps rather from OT:7760 through the idea of definite and conspicuous position; compare OT:8064]; an appellation, as a mark or memorial of individuality; by implication honor, authority, character:

KJV - base, [in-] fame [-ous], named (-d), renown, report.
